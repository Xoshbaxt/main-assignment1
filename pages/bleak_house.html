Bleak House


  Bleak House is a novel by Charles Dickens, first published as a 20-episode serial
between March 1852 and September 1853. The novel has many characters and several subplots,
and is told partly by the novel's heroine, Esther Summerson and partly by an omniscient narrator.
At the centre of Bleak House is a long-running legal case in the Court of Chancery, Jarndyce and
Jarndyce, which comes about because a testator has written several conflicting wills.
