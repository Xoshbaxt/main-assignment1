Oliver Twist


  Oliver Twist; or, The Parish Boy's Progress, is the second novel by English author Charles Dickens.
It was originally published as a serial from 1837 to 1839, and as a three-volume book in 1838. The story
follows the titular orphan, who, after being raised in a workhouse, escapes to London, where he meets a
gang of juvenile pickpockets led by the elderly criminal Fagin, discovers the secrets of his parentage,
and reconnects with his remaining family.
